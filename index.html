📅 About International Mother Language Day 📅

International Mother Language Day is celebrated on February 21st every year. It promotes linguistic and cultural diversity.

Why is it celebrated?

To honor those who fought for language recognition and to promote cultural diversity.

How did it start?

Started in Bangladesh, UNESCO proclaimed it an official day in 1999.

What happens on this day?

People celebrate with events, discussions, and language-based activities.

Why is it celebrated?

  • 🔸 To honor the people of Bangladesh who fought for the recognition of the Bangla language
  • 🔸 To promote the preservation of languages and cultural diversity
  • 🔸 To highlight the importance of multilingual education

How did it start?

  • 🔹 The idea for International Mother Language Day came from Bangladesh
  • 🔹 In 1999, UNESCO proclaimed February 21st as International Mother Language Day
  • 🔹 It was first observed in 2000

What happens on this day?

  • 🌟 People celebrate by organizing seminars and competitions about the history of their language
  • 🌟 People reflect on the importance of respecting and protecting all languages
